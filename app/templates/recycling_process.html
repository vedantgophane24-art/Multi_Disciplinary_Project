The Textile Recycling Process

Turning old clothes into new opportunities.

Why Recycle Textiles?

Every year, tons of clothing and textiles end up in landfills. Recycling not only diverts this waste but also significantly benefits our planet and economy.

It's Sustainable

Recycling textiles saves massive amounts of water, reduces the need for virgin materials like cotton and polyester (which is oil-based), and cuts down on greenhouse gas emissions from landfills.

It's Profitable

A circular economy creates new green jobs. Recycled materials can be sold to manufacturers, and upcycled products (like bags or rugs from old jeans) can be sold directly to consumers online.

  1. 1. Sorting & Grading

    This is the most crucial step. Our team manually sorts every item. Grade A: High-quality, wearable items are cleaned and sold at low cost to our partner NGOs. Grade B/C: Worn-out, stained, or torn items are sent for recycling.

  2. 2. Shredding

    The Grade B/C textiles are put through industrial shredders, which tear the fabric into small scraps. Zippers and buttons are removed, often with magnets.

  3. 3. Cleaning & Carding

    The shredded scraps (now called "shoddy") are thoroughly cleaned. They are then put through a "carding" machine, which combs and untangles the fibers, aligning them into a continuous web.

  4. 4. Spinning & Re-Weaving

    These carded fibers are re-spun into yarn. This new, recycled yarn can be used to weave or knit new fabrics, which will become our future upcycled products.

  5. 5. Other Uses (Downcycling)

    Fibers that are too short to be spun into yarn are "downcycled." They are pressed into new materials, such as insulation for cars, carpet padding, or industrial cleaning rags.
